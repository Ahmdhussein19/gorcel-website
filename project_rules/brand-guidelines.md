## 1. Brand Strategy

### Positioning

The engineering partner MENA businesses turn to when they've outgrown generic software — delivering fixed-scope, full-ownership custom systems with the precision of a global firm and the proximity of a local team.

### Brand Essence

> **Trusted Precision**

Two words that cannot be separated. Trust without precision is goodwill. Precision without trust is cold. Together they define a partner who knows exactly what to do — and does it.

### Personality Archetypes

**Sage + Ruler.** The expert who has already thought through the problem before you finished describing it. The authority who delivers without excuses. Never the salesperson. Always the partner.

### Creative Direction

Architectural minimalism with structural weight. Inspired by blueprints, precision engineering, and the clean rigor of tools like Linear. Dark-anchored palette. Every element earns its place.

### Target Audience

C-suite owners, CTOs, and startup founders at small and mid-size companies across any industry — decision-makers who have outgrown off-the-shelf tools and need a technical partner, not a vendor they have to manage.

### Ambition

Dominant software engineering partner across Egypt and MENA — bringing global engineering standards to businesses that want a partner built to last.

### Controlled Digital Exception

The home page hero may use the supplied woven Three.js particle field as a canvas-only art direction exception. The page surface remains Paper, while particle colors use the reference implementation's light-surface branch: random HSL hue, saturation `0.8`, lightness `0.5`, `NormalBlending`, and opacity `1.0`. This exception is limited to the WebGL particle buffer and does not permit extra colors in CSS, typography, buttons, UI, or static illustration.

## 4. Voice & Tone

### Character

Direct. Confident. Human. Never performative. We speak like a senior engineer who also understands business — someone who has already thought through the problem before you finished describing it.

### The Five Principles

#### 1. Precise Over Polished
Say exactly what you mean. Eliminate decorative language. Every sentence carries information or removes doubt.

✓ **Do:** We deliver working software in short cycles — you see real progress, not status reports.
✗ **Don't:** We leverage agile methodologies to synergize value delivery across stakeholders.

---

#### 2. Partner, Not Vendor
Speak to the client's outcome, not our process. The emphasis is always on what they get and own.

✓ **Do:** You own everything — full code, no lock-in, no recurring license fees on your own product.
✗ **Don't:** Our proprietary methodology ensures maximum throughput and client satisfaction scores.

---

#### 3. Earned Confidence
Never brag. State facts and let them land. Confidence comes from specificity, not superlatives.

✓ **Do:** We agree on fixed scope and cost before writing a line of code.
✗ **Don't:** We're the best software company in MENA, trusted by hundreds of leading businesses.

---

#### 4. Human at Scale
Even technical content is written for a person, not a specification. Warm but never casual. Direct but never cold.

✓ **Do:** If something breaks after launch, we fix it. That's part of the deal.
✗ **Don't:** Post-launch support is available via enterprise-tier SLA packages for qualified accounts.

---

#### 5. Short First, Long When Earned
Default to the shortest version of every sentence. Lead with the conclusion — explain after.

✓ **Do:** We build the software. You own it. Full stop.
✗ **Don't:** In order to ensure that our valued clients are able to retain full intellectual property rights over their digital assets, we structure all of our engagements with comprehensive code ownership provisions built in from day one.

## 5. Iconography & Visuals

### Icon Style
- Outline icons only
- 1.5px stroke weight
- Square caps and joins — zero rounded corners
- 24×24px base grid
- Consistent optical weight at all sizes
- Source: Lucide (square variant) or custom

### Graphic Elements
- Structural grid motifs: thin horizontal and vertical rules at right angles
- Bracket forms used as framing devices
- The accent square used as a precision marker — never decoratively
- No decorative flourishes, textures, or organic shapes

### Illustration Style
- Flat geometric, strictly orthographic — no isometric
- No gradients
- Two-color maximum: Ink + Volt
- Abstract representations of systems and structures — not characters or people

### Photography Direction
- High-contrast, slightly desaturated base
- Subject: real workspaces, screens, hands at work — never stock smile photos
- Color grade: slight cool tone
- Overlay permitted: Ink wash at 40% opacity over photography

### Motion Principles
- Transitions: linear or ease-in-out only — no bounce, no spring
- Duration: 150–250ms
- Elements construct — they do not float or fade
- Interactions feel mechanical, not playful

### Spatial System
- Base unit: 8px — all spacing and sizing in multiples of 8
- Border-radius: 2px maximum on UI elements (cards, tags, inputs)
- Zero radius on marks and logo
- Rule weights: 1px for UI dividers · 2px for emphasis

## 6. Logo System

> **Note:** Logo mark asset to be inserted by the brand owner.

### Mark Concept
Structural G built from negative space and heavy geometric form. Constructed, not drawn. References blueprints, code brackets, and system architecture. The single Volt-colored accent square is the only color carrier in the mark — a precision indicator, the human detail inside the engineered system.

### Lockup Variants

| Variant           | Use |
|-------------------|-----|
| Mark + Wordmark   | Primary — all primary brand touchpoints |
| Mark only         | App icons, favicons, embossed/embroidered applications |
| Wordmark only     | When mark is established context — inline text use |
| Reversed (white)  | On Ink or Onyx backgrounds |
| On Volt           | Ink version only on Volt background |

### Wordmark
- Typeface: DM Sans SemiBold
- Case: Mixed case — `Gorcel` not `GORCEL`
- Letter-spacing: −0.5pt

### Clear Space
Minimum clear space on all four sides = height of the accent square within the mark. No graphic element, text, or image may enter this zone.

### Minimum Size
- Digital: 80px width for full lockup · 24px for mark only
- Print: 20mm width for full lockup · 8mm for mark only

## 7. Usage Rules

### Always Do

1. Use the mark on approved backgrounds only: Paper, Ink, Onyx, Volt.
2. Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the accent square on all four sides.
3. Use the reversed (white) version on dark backgrounds; primary (dark) on light.
4. Use DM Sans for all brand communications. DM Mono for labels, captions, and technical contexts only.
5. Keep Volt as a strict accent — maximum 7% of any composition's visual area.
6. Respect minimum size rules: 80px / 20mm for full lockup, 24px / 8mm for mark only.

### Never Do

1. Never stretch, skew, rotate, or distort the logo mark in any direction.
2. Never apply drop shadows, glows, gradients, or 3D effects to the logo.
3. Never place the logo on a busy photograph without a solid color field beneath it.
4. Never recolor the logo — approved variants only. No blue, red, grey, or custom colors.
5. Never use Volt as a background for large text blocks — legibility degrades.
6. Never use rounded typefaces, decorative fonts, or scripts alongside the brand system.
7. Never set the Gorcel wordmark in all-caps — mixed case only.
